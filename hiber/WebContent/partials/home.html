Welcome to Telangana Technical students welfare association

  The Telangana Technical Students Welfare Association (TTSWA) is a state student's organization created to develop skills in engineering education. TTSWA aims to develop leadership, academic and business management skills in the work place among students and leaders within the community. Over 50,000 engineering college students across 150 engineering colleges and 31 districts are in TTSWA

  Engineering education is the activity of teaching knowledge and principles to the professional practice of engineering. It includes the initial education (Bachelor and or Masters Degree) for becoming an engineer, and any advanced education and specializations that follow. Engineering education is typically accompanied by additional post graduate examinations and supervised training as the requirements for a professional engineering license. The length of education and training to qualify as a basic professional engineer is typical 8 - 12 years with 15 - 20 years for an engineer that takes responsibility for major projects.

  Telangana State is one of the largest producers of engineers in the world. Yet, more than 70% of engineers are unemployed due to severe competition, and by some estimates only 7-8% of engineering graduates are actually employable.

  In Telangana, there are several engineering colleges imparting undergraduate and graduate courses in engineering, applied engineering and Technology
